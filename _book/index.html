Preface

Who is This For?

This series of tutorials are a work in progress. The focal audience includes the members of the Affective and Translational Neuroscience Laboratory at the University of Maryland, headed by Dr. Alexander Shackman. That being said, the contents are open for use and available to anyone looking to survey different analytical approaches to working with psychological and behavioral data that involve repeated/intensive data collection protocols.

Who is Writing This Stuff?

Matthew Barstead, Ph.D.: I graduated from the University of Maryland, receiving my Ph.D. from the Department of Human Development & Quantitative Methodology. I eventually took my talents to the private sector where my full-time job as a Data Scientist and Software Developer keeps me busy from 9 to 5 most days. I started up a side consulting business during my transition out of academia called Dead Reckoning Analytics & Consulting. I chose the name because I used to be a sailing instructor in a prior life and the notion of effective navigation when presented with limited information accurately summarized my lived experience in data science and statistical modeling. I work with clients who need help with data science and modeling projects, adhering to open science/open source practices and principles as often as I can.
